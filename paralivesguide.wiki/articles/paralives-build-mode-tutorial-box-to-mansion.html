Paralives Build Mode Tutorial: From Box to Mansion

Gridless, curved walls, free scaling — these features are revolutionary. But they can also be intimidating. Here's exactly how to go from a basic rectangle to a breathtaking dream home.

📅 Published: May 26, 2026·🔄 Updated: May 26, 2026

🔧 Core Tools — Master These 5 First

1. 🧱 Wall Tool — Your House's Skeleton

Three wall types: Standard (2.4m), Half (1.2m), Invisible. Two shapes: Straight & Curved. Click and drag to draw. Drag arrows to adjust length/height. Always raise your walls to 2.7–3m — the default height feels cramped.

2. 🪵 Floor Tool — More Than Just Fill

Click a room to fill it. Hold Shift to paint a single tile. Create checkerboard, herringbone, or chevron patterns by mixing materials. Use different flooring to visually separate zones (e.g., wood in living room, tile in kitchen).

3. 🚪 Doors & Windows — Resize Everything

Place on walls, then drag corners to resize. Floor-to-ceiling windows? Yes. Tiny accent windows? Also yes. Big house = big windows — they improve lighting and curb appeal.

4. 🏡 Roof Tool — Auto First, Then Tweak

Click Auto Roof for instant coverage. Adjust pitch and eaves by dragging edges. Materials: tile, metal, thatch, slate. Different pitches create different styles — shallow for modern, steep for cottage.

5. 🪜 Stairs — Always Leave Enough Room

Click ground, drag to define length. Supports straight, L‑shape, U‑shape, spiral. Requires at least 3 wide × 4 deep tiles. If placement fails, check ceiling height.

🌀 Gridless Building — True Freedom

Toggle grid with G. Hold Shift to temporarily snap. Rule: grid ON for walls, doors, windows, floors; grid OFF for furniture, decor, details.

Curved Walls — The Signature Feature

Switch to curved wall mode, click and drag. Adjust the radius by dragging control points. Use for: accent living-room walls, circular dining rooms, wavy façades, semi‑circular balconies. Note: curved‑wall windows/doors arrive Q4 2026.

Free‑Angle Placement

In gridless mode, press R to rotate freely. Hold Shift + R for 1‑degree micro‑adjustments. Angle a sofa toward the TV, tilt chairs around a table — your rooms will feel lived‑in, not robotic.

Split‑Levels & Vaulted Ceilings

Use the Platform Tool to raise/lower floor sections. A raised living room or sunken dining area adds instant sophistication. Remove part of the second story for a dramatic double‑height ceiling.

⚠️ Keep platform height differences under 0.5 m, otherwise Paras can't walk onto them.

🎨 Item Customization — Every Piece Unique

Free Scaling: press [/]. Enlarge a side table into a dining table, a small rug into a room‑sized carpet, a single bed into a double. Full Color Wheel: pick any hue + paste hex codes for exact matching. Texture Swap: change wood to marble, fabric to leather. Eyedropper (P): copy color; hold Shift to clone size and rotation too. Object Stacking: place books, cups, vases on tables — no cheat codes needed. Toggle “Ignore Collision” for overlapping rugs or layered plants.

🏠 Interior Layout — Comfort First

  • 🛋️ Living Room (min 4×5m): Face sofa toward TV, leave 2m between. L‑shaped sofa + round coffee table + TV unit is foolproof.
  • 🛏️ Bedroom (min 3×4m): Bed not facing the door. Leave 0.5m on each side for nightstands. Keep furniture minimal.
  • 🍳 Kitchen (min 3×3m): Follow the “wash‑chop‑cook” workflow: sink → counter → stove. Fridge near entrance. At least 1.5m of counter space.
  • 🚿 Bathroom (min 2×2m): Separate wet and dry zones. Vanity near door, toilet middle, shower at back.

🚫 Top 10 Beginner Mistakes

  1. Wall height too low — raise to 2.7–3m.
  2. Windows too small or too few — bigger windows = better light.
  3. Stair space miscalculation — need at least 3×4 tiles.
  4. Furniture too large — overscaled items block paths.
  5. Too many colors — stick to 3 hues per room.
  6. Ignoring lighting — use ceiling + task + accent lights.
  7. No functional zones — define areas with rugs or half‑walls.
  8. Bad traffic flow — ensure clear walkways between rooms.
  9. Forgetting to save — Ctrl+S often.
  10. Starting too big — master a one‑bedroom before attempting a mansion.

🛤️ Your Build Journey Roadmap

🏠 House 1: simple one‑bedroom box — learn every tool.
🏡 House 2: two‑bedroom with curved walls and a split‑level.
🌳 House 3: cottage with a landscaped yard.
🏰 House 4: modern mega‑build with complex structure and lighting.

Keep experimenting, watch community speed‑builds, and most importantly — have fun creating!
