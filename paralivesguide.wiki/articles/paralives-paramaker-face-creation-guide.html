Paralives Paramaker Guide: Master Face Creation Like a Pro

After sculpting over 20 Paras in early access, I'm convinced Paramaker is the best character creator in any life sim. Here's exactly how to make faces that look unique and gorgeous.

📅 Published: May 22, 2026·🔄 Updated: May 22, 2026

🎨 Before You Start

  • Find a reference photo — a celebrity or real person — to guide your creation.
  • Reset all sliders to middle — start from a blank canvas, not the random preset.
  • Switch to side view frequently — many beginners only work in front view, resulting in odd profiles.

🏗️ Base Face Shape (Most Important)

Always sculpt the overall head shape before touching eyes or nose.

Overall Proportions

Use the "Three Courts, Five Eyes" rule: the face is divided into three equal vertical segments (hairline to brow, brow to nose base, nose base to chin) and is roughly five eye-widths wide.

Jawline & Chin

Adjust jaw width and angle for round, square, heart, or diamond face shapes. Chin length and shape (pointed, round, square) dramatically change the character.

Forehead & Cheekbones

Cheekbone height and width influence whether the face looks sharp or soft. Balance them with the jaw.

👁️ Facial Features: Eyes, Nose, Mouth

Position matters more than shape. Get the placement right first.

Eyes

  • Eyes sit at the midpoint of the head vertically.
  • Distance between eyes ≈ one eye's width.
  • Avoid oversized eyes for a natural look.
  • Don't forget details: eyelashes, eyelids, eye bags, and catchlights.

Nose

  • Length ≈ distance from brow to nose base.
  • Bridge height and tip shape (pointed, round, aquiline) add character.

Mouth

  • Mouth sits slightly above the midpoint between nose base and chin.
  • Width ≈ distance between pupils.
  • Upper-to-lower lip ratio about 1:1.5.

💇 Hair & Makeup

Hair

Paramaker splits hair into root, mid-length, tips, and highlights — each can have a different color. Adjust length, volume, and bangs to frame the face. Match hairstyle to face shape: round faces benefit from layered long hair; square faces from soft waves; long faces from shoulder-length cuts.

Makeup

  • Foundation: match skin tone, avoid too pale.
  • Blush: apples of cheeks for youth; higher on cheekbones for maturity.
  • Lipstick: choose shades and finishes that fit the vibe.
  • Freckles & moles: add realism and uniqueness.

💎 Pro Paramaker Tips

  • Harness the full color wheel: paste hex codes for precise skin, eye, and hair colors.
  • Embrace asymmetry: real faces aren't perfectly mirrored. Slightly tweak one eye or eyebrow for naturalism.
  • Save presets: store your best noses, eyes, or full faces to reuse later.
  • Reference real people, not just cartoons: your Para will look more believable.

Paramaker rewards patience. Experiment, iterate, and don't be afraid to start over. Your masterpiece is just a few slider tweaks away.
