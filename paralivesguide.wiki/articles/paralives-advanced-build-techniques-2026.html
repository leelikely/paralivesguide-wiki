Paralives Advanced Build Techniques: Curves, Split‑Levels & Lighting

Take your houses from basic boxes to stunning showpieces. Learn curved walls, split‑levels, custom stairs, professional lighting, and outdoor landscaping — all with native tools.

📅 Published: May 27, 2026·🔄 Updated: May 27, 2026
⚠️ Early Access Limitations: Curved‑wall windows/doors aren't supported yet (coming Q4 2026). Pools are visual only; swimming arrives later this year.

🌀 Curved Walls & Circular Rooms

Curved walls instantly elevate a build. Press G to disable the grid, then select the Curved Wall tool. Click and drag to draw an arc; drag the center control point to adjust the radius. Straight and curved walls snap together seamlessly.

Perfect Circles with Zero Gaps

Combine four identical 90‑degree curved wall segments end‑to‑end. I used this technique for a circular sunroom surrounded by oversized floor‑to‑ceiling windows — stunning natural light.

Design Ideas & Pitfalls

  • 🛋️ Curved living‑room backdrop: a gentle arc behind the sofa creates a luxurious focal point.
  • 🌊 Wavy exterior façade: alternate convex and concave segments for a dynamic silhouette.
  • ❌ Avoid: placing large furniture directly against curved walls — it often blocks pathing. Also, don't stack curved walls on different floors with mismatched radii; it can cause collision bugs.

🏗️ Split‑Levels & Vaulted Ceilings

The Platform Tool (T) lets you raise or lower floor sections, adding instant sophistication. Keep height differences under 0.6 m so Paras can walk without stairs.

Sunken Living Room (Beginner‑Friendly)

Select the platform tool, draw a zone, and drag the arrow down 0.5 m. I sink the living area to naturally separate it from the dining space.

Raised Entryway

Lift the entrance 0.3 m and surround it with low railings — instantly defines the transition from outdoors to indoors.

Dramatic Double‑Height Lounge

Remove the second‑story floor above the living room. Raise all walls to 3 m. Leave a 1.5 m‑wide gallery on the second floor so you can overlook the space below. Suspend a large chandelier in the void — instant mansion vibes.

🪜 Custom Stairs & Railings

Don't settle for default straight stairs. Paralives lets you freely adjust angle, width, and turn.

  • Spiral staircases: ideal for tight corners; scale the diameter to fit.
  • Diagonal stairs: place at 45° to align with curved or angled walls.
  • Mini‑steps: for small 0.3–0.5 m level changes, use a single‑step transition instead of a full staircase.
  • Parallel double stairs: leave a slim atrium between two staircases for a grand, light‑filled entry.

Railings are fully customizable — resize, recolor, mix half‑walls with glass panels. Use transparent railings for uninterrupted views, or combine a half‑wall base with metal spindles for balconies.

💡 Layered Lighting — The Soul of a Room

90% of beginner builds look flat because they rely on a single ceiling light. Paralives' lighting system supports brightness, color temperature, and radius adjustments. Follow the three‑layer rule:

  1. Ambient base: recessed or pendant lights set to 40% brightness, warm white.
  2. Accent layers: wall sconces behind the TV, strip lights under stairs, linear LEDs in high ceilings.
  3. Task lighting: focused spots over kitchen counters, reading lamps on desks, vanity lights.

Room‑specific setups: Living room = ambient + floor lamp + TV‑backlight. Bedroom = turn off the main light; use bedside lamps and recessed spots. Kitchen = bright cool‑white task lights. Vaulted lounge = a suspended chandelier pulls the eye upward.

If hotspots appear, reduce individual light radii so beams don't overlap excessively.

🌳 Outdoor Landscaping & Pool Scenery

Start by flattening the terrain around the house, then sculpt gentle slopes for pathways. Layer vegetation: tall trees, mid‑height shrubs, and ground‑cover flowers. Repeat shapes: if your house has curved walls, design curved flowerbeds.

Placeholder Pool

Even though swimming isn't ready, you can build the pool shell now:

  • Dig a basin with the terrain tool, shape it square or organic.
  • Line walls with blue waterproof tiles, floor with light‑blue wave texture.
  • Surround with glass railings, sun loungers, umbrellas, and outdoor tables.
  • Add underwater mood lights — they glow beautifully at night.

Add a dining terrace with a large table, a BBQ corner, or a fire‑pit seating area. Soft string lights overhead complete the evening ambiance.

⌨️ Essential Hotkeys & Common Pitfalls

  • G — toggle grid
  • Shift + rotate — 1‑degree fine rotation
  • Page Up/Page Down — switch floors
  • Ctrl+Z — undo
  • F5 — quick save (use it constantly!)

❌ Top 5 Mistakes: forcing windows onto curved walls (causes visual glitches), making indoor level changes >0.6 m without stairs, over‑brightening lights, planting too many trees (kills FPS), and leaving attic galleries narrower than 1 m.

Paralives' building ceiling is sky‑high. Experiment fearlessly, study community speed‑builds, and you'll soon be crafting dream homes that leave others speechless.
