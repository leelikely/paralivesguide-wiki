3 Trendy Paralives House Builds (Step by Step)

Ready to go beyond boxes? Follow these detailed blueprints for a curved-wall showpiece, a cozy attic retreat, and a luxury infinity pool — all in Paralives.

📅 Published: May 22, 2026·🔄 Updated: May 22, 2026

🏛️ 1. Modern Curved-Wall Living Room

This signature Paralives design uses the curved wall tool to create a stunning focal point.

📐 Requirements: Lot size 20×20 · Budget ~20,000 Paradimes · Difficulty: Intermediate

Step 1 Draw the Base

Use straight walls to draw a 10×8 rectangle. Switch to the Curved Wall Tool and draw a half-circle (radius 4 tiles) on one short side. Delete the original straight wall behind it. You now have a room with one stunning curved façade.

Step 2 Install Floor-to-Ceiling Windows

On the curved wall, place three large windows spanning from floor to ceiling. Use the Alt key for precise placement. On the opposite straight wall, place a grand double door; on the side walls, add smaller windows for balanced light.

Step 3 Flooring & Walls

Paint the floor with light-gray wood. Apply white paint to the curved wall and soft gray to the remaining three walls. Add a white crown molding along the top edge of the curved wall for a polished look.

Step 4 Furnish Like a Pro

Place an L-shaped sofa facing the curved windows. Add a round coffee table in the center with a vase and books. Across from the sofa, set a TV stand. Fill the corner with a floor lamp and a tall potted plant.

Step 5 Lighting Layers

Install recessed ceiling lights across the room. Place a floor lamp beside the sofa and a small table lamp on the TV stand. The curved window wall provides natural light during daytime hours.

🏠 2. Cozy Sloped Attic Bedroom

Create the ultimate cozy hideaway with a dramatic sloped ceiling.

📐 Requirements: Lot size 15×15 · Budget ~15,000 Paradimes · Difficulty: Advanced

Step 1 Build the First Floor

Draw a 12×10 rectangle. Divide it into a living area, kitchen, and bathroom. Install basic doors, windows, and essential furniture.

Step 2 Second-Story Frame

Go to the second floor and draw walls along the outline of the first floor. Delete the front and back walls, leaving only the left and right side walls standing.

Step 3 Create the Sloped Roof

Select the Mono-Pitch Roof. Drag from the top of the front wall down to the top of the back wall. Set the pitch to approximately 30 degrees. Add a couple of skylight windows directly into the roof for charm.

Step 4 Divide the Attic Space

Use half-walls to separate the attic into a sleeping zone and a reading nook. Place a double bed with nightstands in the bedroom area, and a desk, chair, and bookshelf in the study corner.

Step 5 Warm Up the Decor

Floor: natural oak wood. Sloped ceiling: white. Walls: soft beige. String fairy lights along the roof beam for a cozy glow. Add a thick rug, throw pillows, and a reading lamp.

🏖️ 3. Resort-Style Infinity-Pool Backyard

Bring vacation vibes home with a sleek infinity pool overlooking a lush landscape.

📐 Requirements: Lot size 30×30 · Budget ~30,000 Paradimes · Difficulty: Advanced

Step 1 Terrain Preparation

Flatten the backyard area. Leave a flat section close to the house for a deck. Using the Terrain Lower Tool, drop the land one tile beyond the deck to create a natural basin for the pool.

Step 2 Build the Pool

Select the Pool Tool and draw a 10×6 rectangle in the lowered area. Set depth to about 1.5 meters. Line the interior with light-blue mosaic tile and the coping with white stone.

Step 3 Infinity Edge Illusion

On the far side of the pool (away from the house), raise the terrain close to the pool wall and reduce the pool wall height until it's flush with the water surface. Plant tall shrubs and tropical greenery behind it. From the house, the water will appear to merge with the sky.

Step 4 Deck & Lounge Area

Cover the flat area between the house and pool with wooden deck tiles. Place an outdoor dining table, chairs, a market umbrella, and two sun loungers. Add potted palms along the deck edge.

Step 5 Final Touches

Scatter colorful floats in the pool. String bistro lights over the deck. Place a barbecue grill and an outdoor mini-fridge. Add a hammock between two trees for the ultimate relaxation spot.

💡 Pro Building Advice: Work from reference photos. Don't strive for perfection in the first pass — build the overall structure, then go back and tweak details. Save multiple copies as you progress so you can revert if needed.
