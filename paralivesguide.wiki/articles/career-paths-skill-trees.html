Career Paths & Skill Trees Overview

Every career ranked and explained — salaries, requirements, promotions, and the skill synergies that will make or break your Para's professional life.

📅 Published: January 22, 2026·🔄 Updated: January 22, 2026

Overview

Here's something I learned the hard way: in Paralives, your career isn't just a money faucet you turn on and forget about. It's a major part of your Para's identity. The job you pick determines your daily schedule, which skills you'll be developing, who you meet, and even how other Paras perceive you in social situations.

I've now played through most of the career paths at least once, and I've made plenty of mistakes along the way (turns out "Artist" is a terrible choice if your Para has zero creativity traits — who knew?). This guide breaks down every career, how skills feed into them, and which combinations will set you up for success.

💡 Tip: You can switch careers at any time, but you'll start from the bottom of the new career ladder. Promotions in your old career don't transfer. Think of it like real life — quitting your job as a Level 8 Engineer to become a Level 1 Musician means taking a massive pay cut.

How Careers Work

Careers in Paralives follow a 10-level progression system with branching paths at certain levels. Here's the basic structure:

  • Levels 1-3: Entry level. Low pay, basic tasks, but you're learning the ropes.
  • Levels 4-6: Mid-career. Pay improves significantly. You'll unlock career-specific interactions and perks.
  • Level 7-8: Branch point. Most careers offer two specializations here — pick one and your role changes permanently.
  • Levels 9-10: Top of the field. Maximum salary, exclusive perks, and serious social status.

To get promoted, you need to meet three requirements each time: a minimum skill level in one or two relevant skills, a certain number of "career tasks" completed (these vary by job — could be writing reports, painting pieces, fixing objects, etc.), and maintaining good job performance by showing up on time and in a decent mood.

Full Career List

Here's every career available at launch, along with my honest take on each one.

🎨 Artist

Skills needed: Painting, Creativity
Starting salary: Low · Max salary: High (if you hit it big)
Branches: Fine Artist (gallery sales, exhibitions) or Commercial Illustrator (steady freelance work, more reliable income)

The Artist career is a slow burn. Early levels pay terribly — you'll be making more money selling paintings on the side than from your actual job. But if you stick with it, the top levels are lucrative and deeply satisfying. The "Masterpiece" mechanic at Level 8+ can produce paintings worth thousands. Pair with the Creative trait for faster skill gains.

💻 Tech & Programming

Skills needed: Programming, Logic
Starting salary: Medium · Max salary: Very High
Branches: Software Developer (corporate, high salary) or Indie Game Dev (lower pay but creative freedom and occasional windfalls)

If you want a straightforward path to wealth, this is it. The Tech career has the best salary-to-effort ratio in the game. Programming skill also unlocks useful abilities like upgrading your computer, hacking (mostly harmless fun), and building apps as side income. The Indie Game Dev branch is more volatile — you might release a hit and make a fortune, or you might release a flop and barely scrape by. Risk versus reward.

🍳 Culinary

Skills needed: Cooking, Creativity
Starting salary: Low · Max salary: Medium-High
Branches: Chef (restaurant kitchens, high pressure) or Food Critic (travel, writing, more flexible hours)

The Culinary career is underrated. Cooking skill benefits your entire household — your Para can make higher-quality meals that give better mood buffs. At higher levels, you unlock exclusive recipes that you can't learn any other way. The Chef branch has stressful hours but brings prestige; the Food Critic branch is chiller and lets you work from home more often.

🏥 Medical

Skills needed: Logic, Charisma
Starting salary: Medium · Max salary: High
Branches: Doctor (hospital-based, long hours) or Medical Researcher (lab-based, more predictable schedule)

The Medical career demands a lot of your Para's time — long shifts, on-call emergencies, and constant skill maintenance. But it's one of the most respected careers socially, and it unlocks unique interactions like diagnosing other Paras and giving health advice. The Researcher branch is better if you want work-life balance.

🎵 Music & Entertainment

Skills needed: Music (instrument-specific), Charisma
Starting salary: Low-Medium · Max salary: High (with fame)
Branches: Performer (concerts, touring) or Composer (studio work, royalties)

This career is tied to the Fame system, which deserves its own guide. Short version: the more famous you get, the more money you make, but the more your private life gets invaded by fans and paparazzi. The Composer branch is the "quiet genius" path — less fame, but steady royalty income that can continue even if you switch careers later.

✍️ Writing & Journalism

Skills needed: Writing, Research
Starting salary: Low · Max salary: Medium-High
Branches: Novelist (book deals, creative control) or Journalist (steady work, breaking stories)

Writing is the ultimate flexible career. You can work from home, set your own hours, and the skill itself lets you write books that generate passive royalty income forever. Even if you switch to another career later, those books keep earning. The Journalist path is more structured but gives you unique access to in-game events.

🛠️ Skilled Trades

Skills needed: Handiness, Logic
Starting salary: Medium · Max salary: Medium-High
Branches: Electrician (specialized, high demand) or General Contractor (variety of jobs)

Not the flashiest career, but incredibly practical. The Handiness skill saves you money on repairs, lets you upgrade appliances, and makes you self-sufficient. Your Para can fix their own stuff instead of paying for service calls, which adds up over time.

🔬 Science

Skills needed: Logic, Research
Starting salary: Medium · Max salary: High
Branches: Academic (university, grants) or Inventor (gadgets, patents)

The Inventor branch is one of the most fun career paths in the game. You get to build actual gadgets that your Para can use — teleporters, cleaning bots, mood-enhancing devices. It requires a lot of tinkering and resource gathering, but the payoff is tangible. The Academic branch is calmer and better for skill-focused players.

Skill Trees Explained

Skills in Paralives aren't just numbers that go up — they're trees with unlockable perks at certain levels. Each skill has about 10-15 unlockable abilities spread across its progression. Here's how the skill system works in general:

  • Levels 1-3: Basic competency. You can do the thing, but slowly and with mistakes.
  • Levels 4-6: Proficiency. Faster, fewer failures, unlock quality improvements.
  • Levels 7-8: Expertise. Unlock unique recipes/techniques/moves. Other Paras start seeking you out for help.
  • Levels 9-10: Mastery. Maximum efficiency. Unlock legendary-tier abilities unique to that skill.

Skills improve through practice and study. You can read skill books (slow but steady), take classes (faster, costs money), or learn by doing (varies by activity). The fastest method is usually a combination — read a beginner book to get to Level 3, then practice actively, then take an advanced class to push past a plateau.

Beyond the Day Job: Making Money

Your career salary is just one income stream. Smart players diversify:

  • Side hustles: Freelance gigs based on your skills. Programmers can take coding contracts, artists can do commissions, writers can sell short pieces to magazines.
  • Passive income: Book royalties, painting sales (keep them in your inventory to appreciate in value!), rental properties (if you own multiple lots).
  • Crafting for profit: Woodworking, cooking, and invention can all produce sellable goods. The markup is highest on unique or high-quality items.
  • Investments: At higher Logic levels, you unlock the ability to invest in the stock market. It's slow and requires capital, but it's truly passive income.

Best Trait + Career Combos

After testing various combinations, here are the synergies that gave me the smoothest rides:

  • Creative + Perfectionist → Artist: Faster painting skill gains + higher quality paintings. You'll cruise through promotions.
  • Genius + Foodie → Tech: Logic bonus from Genius accelerates programming skill. Foodie keeps your mood up during long coding sessions.
  • Outgoing + Creative → Music: Charisma from Outgoing helps with performances and networking. Creative feeds into songwriting.
  • Neat + Genius → Medical: Medical careers demand precision. Neat keeps your environment clean (better focus), Genius speeds up Logic gains.

Pro Tips

  1. Read the job description carefully. Some careers have hidden requirements — like needing a certain number of friends or a specific object (easel, instrument, etc.) at home.
  2. Don't neglect social tasks. Many promotions require socializing with coworkers or networking. If you picked the Loner trait, this can be genuinely challenging.
  3. Mood matters. Going to work in a terrible mood reduces your performance and can delay promotions by days. Eat well, sleep enough, and maybe take a relaxing bath before your shift.
  4. The first promotion is usually the hardest. Once you break into Level 4 territory, the momentum picks up. Don't get discouraged early.
  5. Skills are permanent, careers aren't. If you max out a skill and then switch careers, that skill is still maxed. A Para who mastered Painting as an Artist can pivot to a completely different career and will always have that artistic ability in their back pocket.

What's Next?

Pick a career that matches your Para's traits and your preferred playstyle. Don't overthink it — you can always change paths later. And if you discover a career combo that works amazingly well, tell us about it! We're always looking for new strategies to share with the community.
